Parks & Rec Asset Inventory

Capture every park, trail, facility, and field.

Capture parks by walking, cart, truck, or bike — with longer-reach mounts for larger areas. GeoCam creates and refreshes a GIS-ready visual asset inventory across every park, trail, facility, and field.

The data flows into ArcGIS and asset systems so teams can plan maintenance, document condition, prioritize work orders, and defend budgets with spatial evidence.

Why it matters

Most parks teams do not have a complete, current, photo-backed asset inventory. They know the obvious assets, but not condition, exact location, ADA issues, deferred maintenance, or lifecycle evidence. GeoCam turns a park into a measurable, repeatable, 4D asset record.

FAQ

Common Questions

How long does it take to capture a campus or parks system?

Depends on size and scope. A small college campus or a group of parks and trails can often be piloted in 1-2 weeks. We can scale capture teams for larger universities, recreation systems, or tighter timelines.

Can we capture walking and biking trails?

Yes. GeoCam supports drive mode for roads and parking, bike or cart capture for trails and park paths, walk mode for tight areas, and indoor walk mode for building interiors. All data links together in one platform.

Can we document facilities located on or near trails?

Yes. Trailheads, shelters, restrooms, bridges, signs, lighting, water stations, parking, and nearby recreation buildings can all be captured as part of the same site record.

How do we keep data current after repairs or upgrades?

Re-capture specific areas after renovations, trail repairs, new amenities, or park upgrades. Change detection highlights differences, so your digital twin evolves with your physical sites.

Can visitors use this for wayfinding or virtual tours?

Yes. Generate shareable links for self-guided campus, park, facility, and trail exploration. It is useful for admissions, orientation, accessibility planning, and visitor-facing recreation maps.

How do we get started?

Start with a 1-week sprint. Pilot a section of campus, a park, a trail corridor, or a recreation facility to validate the workflow before scaling.
